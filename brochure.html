Welcome. We are so glad you are here.

Whether you are just beginning your journey along the coast, or gently bringing it to a close, we are truly delighted to have you with us. Here, on the pristine shores of Diani Beach, we hope you will find a place to pause, to breathe, and to simply feel at home.

Soroi Blue Diani is a small property, and we like it that way. Our team knows most guests by name within a day. If there is anything you need — a restaurant recommendation, a boat trip arranged, a quiet place to read — please ask. We mean that genuinely.

This guide has everything you need to find your way around, make the most of the coast, and reach us whenever you need us. Beyond that, all you need to do is relax.

Karibu sana — you are most welcome.

Our Commitment to the Coast

Reef Protection

Please take care not to touch or stand on coral while snorkelling or swimming. A healthy reef is a living reef — please do not collect shells or sea life.

Zero Single-Use Plastic

We are committed to eliminating single-use plastic from the property. Refillable water bottles are provided in every room — please use them.

Marine & Wildlife

Please admire all marine life from a respectful distance. Feeding or handling animals is harmful and actively discouraged along this coastline.

Beach vendors and hawkers operate independently from Soroi Blue. Let our team know if you feel uncomfortable — we are always here to help you.

Beyond the Property

Discover & Drift

01 At the Coast
Signature Experience

Diving & Snorkelling

Kenya's south coast reef is one of the most varied in East Africa — warm, clear, and alive year-round. Dive Kisite-Mpunguti Marine Park or snorkel directly off the beach. Both experiences are extraordinary, and accessible to all levels. Ask reception for current operators and the best tides to go.

The coast beyond the property offers one of East Africa's most varied menus of activity — from deep-sea fishing to sacred forest walks. The following pages cover everything available. Our concierge team can arrange any of it.

Beyond the Property — At the Coast

02 Nature & Culture

Colobus Conservation

The Diani forest is home to one of Kenya's rarest primates. The conservation centre offers guided walks and close encounters with the resident wildlife.

Kaya Kinondo Sacred Forest

An ancient forest, centuries old and spiritually alive. Guided walks with local Digo community members reveal stories the trees have kept for generations.

Kongo Mosque

One of the oldest mosques on the Kenyan coast — a window into the deep Swahili history of this shoreline. Modest dress required.

Community Initiatives

Conservation projects operate all along this stretch of coast. Ask the manager if you'd like to contribute during your stay.

Beyond the Property — Day Trips

Day Trips from Diani

03 Day Trips from Diani
Wasini Island & Kisite

A full day by dhow — snorkelling, dolphins in open water, and a long Swahili seafood lunch on the island.

Shimba Hills National Park

Forest elephants, rare roan antelope, and the Sheldrick Falls hike. A completely different landscape from the coast.

Fort Jesus & Old Town

A UNESCO World Heritage Site. Carved doors, spice markets, and the layered history of the Swahili coast.

Glass Bottom Boat

Explore the reef aboard a traditional dhow — no diving experience needed. Spectacular coral views.

Markets & Shopping

Diani Shopping Centre, Carrefour, and craft markets. All within 10–15 minutes — ask for a map.

Fire Safety — What to Do

1

Alert

Raise the alarm. Shout "Fire!" and activate the nearest alarm point. Call reception: Dial 0.

2

Evacuate

Leave immediately. Do not use lifts. Close all doors behind you as you go.

3

Assembly

Proceed to the assembly point at the main car park. Do not re-enter the building.

4

Emergency

Kenya Fire & Rescue: 999 or 112. Please follow all staff instructions calmly.
